FAQs about William Bonner

Who is William Bonner?

William Bonner, born William Bonemer Júnior on November 16, 1963, is a prominent Brazilian journalist, newscaster, and editor. He’s best known for his role as the editor-in-chief and anchor of Jornal Nacional, the most-watched news program in Brazil, aired by TV Globo.

What is he known for?

Bonner is widely recognized for his work on Jornal Nacional, where he has been the lead anchor since 1996. His influential presence and authoritative delivery have made him a respected figure in Brazilian journalism.

What is his educational background?

He graduated with a degree in Mass Communication, emphasizing Advertising and Publicity, from the School of Arts and Communication at the University of São Paulo (ECA-USP).

How did William Bonner start his career?

Bonner began his career in advertising in 1983. He transitioned to broadcasting in 1985, starting at Radio USPFM, and then moved to TV Bandeirantes as an announcer and host. By 1986, he joined TV Globo, where he steadily rose through the ranks.
